# Insights Growth Playbook

Owner: Insights Growth Steward  
Supporting agents: Content Director, Website Steward, Brand Strategist, Proof and Governance Auditor, Distribution & Analytics Agent.

## Purpose

[Recommendation] Use this playbook to keep Kramaniti Insights useful as a connected growth asset without overloading the recurring publisher automation.

The publisher still owns drafting, validation, commit, and push. The Insights Growth Steward owns the editorial-growth checks that make each post easier to navigate, cite, and connect to the wider Kramaniti system.

## Per-Post Checks

### 1. Internal Contextual Links

[Recommendation] Every new post should include 2-3 contextual internal links when the article naturally points to existing Kramaniti routes.

Use meaningful anchor text inside the body, not naked URLs or generic text. Prefer highlighted anchors when the article renderer supports it:

```html
<a href="/#services"><gold>Systems Engineering</gold></a>
```

Good internal route candidates:

- `/` for the overall Kramaniti method when the article explains strategy before tools, systems before scale, or content after clarity.
- `/#method` for method, sequence, or operating philosophy.
- `/#services` for Foundation Strategy, Systems Engineering, or Complete Lifecycle Retainer.
- `/#workflows` for process, diagnosis, implementation, adoption, and continuous improvement.
- `/#contact` only when the paragraph directly invites an audit or next step.
- `/founder` for founder-led clarity, judgment, story, or Karan's operating lens.
- `/work` for selected work as a general proof route.
- `/work/nexocean` for internal workflow tools, recruiter support tooling, or brand-content delivery examples.
- `/work/maitri` for consumer-facing spatial, character, education, or immersive experience references.
- `/insights` for the full archive when the article refers to the broader editorial system.
- `/insights/<slug>` for specific related Insights when one article extends or contrasts another.

Do not add internal links only for SEO density. The link must help the reader move to a relevant next context.

### 2. Source Freshness Rule

[Recommendation] Prefer current primary or near-primary sources for market movement, product changes, regulations, AI capabilities, adoption data, and live platform guidance.

Freshness defaults:

- For AI capability, product, platform, legal, regulatory, market, or pricing claims, prefer sources from the last 24 months.
- For Indian market or sector-specific claims, prefer current local primary sources when available.
- For standards, methods, and durable frameworks, evergreen sources are acceptable when they remain canonical and stronger than a newer commentary piece.
- For older evergreen sources, briefly preserve why they are used: standard, canonical framework, official documentation, or stable method.

Never fabricate a freshness claim. If source recency cannot be verified, either omit the claim or state the source limitation in the run summary.

### 3. Soft CTA Match

[Recommendation] Each article should close with a soft CTA that fits the category and does not turn the article into a sales page.

Category fit:

- `Strategy`: invite an AI workflow audit or strategic clarity conversation.
- `Systems`: invite a systems route review.
- `Adoption`: invite an adoption, handoff, or human-review design conversation.
- `Governance`: invite proof, policy, claim, or review-route mapping.
- `Content`: invite content infrastructure or source-route design.
- `AI Infrastructure`: invite infrastructure scope and operating-boundary review.
- `Spatial`: invite experience-system or presence-route mapping.

The article template can render these CTAs automatically. The post body should still make the CTA feel earned through the article's argument.

### 4. Cluster Posts

[Recommendation] After every 4-6 successful posts, or at least once per month if enough material exists, check whether 3-5 related Insights should become a cluster or pillar article.

Cluster article requirements:

- It must synthesize related posts, not merely list them.
- It should link to 3-5 existing Insights with descriptive anchor text.
- It should have a distinct title, thesis, and category.
- It should preserve source discipline by reusing verified source context or adding new verified primary sources where needed.
- It should help readers understand a larger Kramaniti theme such as workflow alignment, adoption support, proof routing, operating documentation, or content after clarity.

Cluster posts should count as normal Insights publish runs and must pass the same validation, SEO, commit, and push contract.

## Publish Memory Fields

After a successful publish, record these fields in the automation memory in addition to the existing publish notes:

- Internal links added or omitted, with reason.
- Source freshness decision.
- CTA category and rendered CTA route.
- Cluster-post status: not due, candidate identified, or cluster published.

## Guardrails

- Do not claim traction, ranking, backlinks, leads, or conversion impact unless analytics evidence was inspected in the current run.
- Do not add client names, testimonials, metrics, logos, or project outcomes unless proof and permission are cleared.
- Do not overload a post with links. Useful reader pathways matter more than link count.
- Do not replace `sourceLinks`; inline links and internal links are additive.
- Do not let SEO goals override Kramaniti's core sequence: strategy before tools, systems before scale, content after clarity.
