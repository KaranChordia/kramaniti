# Analytics Log

**Purpose:** Track what Kramaniti publishes, where it was distributed, what happened, and what should be repeated, revised, or stopped.

**Key Findings:** [Recommendation] Early analytics should focus on qualitative learning and signal quality before over-optimizing for volume metrics.

**Evidence:** `04_content_system/calendar/content_calendar.md`, `04_content_system/distribution/publishing_checklist.md`, and founder-approved publishing records.

**Interpretation:** [Inference] The useful feedback loop is not just performance. It is whether the content improves clarity, attracts the right conversations, and supports the offer ladder.

**Implications:** The Distribution & Analytics Agent owns the log, but it should not invent metrics or infer business outcomes without evidence.

**Open Questions:** Which platform metrics are available and approved to track?

**Next Steps:** Add a row after each approved public post, newsletter, article, or campaign asset.

## Signal Types

| Signal | Use |
| :--- | :--- |
| Replies or comments | Check whether the idea created useful conversation |
| Saves or shares | Check whether the content was useful enough to keep |
| Qualified inquiries | Check whether content supported the business pipeline |
| Website visits | Check whether distribution drove exploration |
| Founder notes | Capture what felt aligned, difficult, or worth repeating |

## Review Cadence

- Weekly: update rows for recently published assets.
- Monthly: identify patterns to repeat, revise, or stop.
- Quarterly: compare content themes against business pipeline and website direction.
